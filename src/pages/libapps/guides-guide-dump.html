Library Catalogs

Below are some starting points for searching or locating catalogs for libraries around the world.  If you need help finding a library catalog for a specific institution abroad, please contact a librarian.

  • WorldCat (OCLC) This link opens in a new window
    WorldCat is a database that allows researchers to search the combined catalogs of hundreds of libraries around the world. It contains more than 52 million records for books, journals, audiovisual materials and more. This source can help researchers find items, verify citations, and identify which libraries hold a particular title.
  • European Libraries This link opens in a new window
    The Conference of European National Librarians (CENL). A portal to the print and digital resources of 47 national libraries of the member states of the Council of Europe. Catalogs of more than 250 collections are searchable or browsable. Federated searching permits the user to specify which collections are searched.
  • LibWeb This link opens in a new window
    From the Berkeley Digital Library, a list of library catalogs and websites online.
  • List of National Libraries (Wikipedia) This link opens in a new window
    Visit the websites for specific national libraries for access to their catalogs, collection databases, and finding aids.

Archives

Below are some resources to help you identify collections in major archives in other countries. However, many smaller archives will not have searchable finding aids or guides, or may have very limited information about collections on their website. Occasionally, there are print bibliographies or guides listing collections in specific archives. You can search for these in the Library Catalog or WorldCat. But often, you may have to reach out to the archive directly for more information.

  • ArchiveGrid This link opens in a new window
    Thousands of libraries, museums, and archives have contributed nearly a million collection descriptions to ArchiveGrid. Researchers searching ArchiveGrid can learn about the many items in each of these collections, contact archives to arrange a visit to examine materials, and order copies.
  • ProQuest Dissertations & Theses This link opens in a new window
    Includes citations for materials from the first U.S. dissertation (1861) to those accepted as recently as last semester. Starting in 1997 full-text is often available. If full-text is not available information about ordering the document is provided.
  • British Library Archives and Manuscripts This link opens in a new window
    & more less...
    Search here to view details of the British Library's archive and manuscript collections.
    Not all records for these collections are online, so be sure to consult the BL's other archive and manuscripts catalogues.
  • List of National Archives (Wikipedia) This link opens in a new window
    List of central archives maintained by various countries, including links to websites, where available.
  • Fresh From the Archives This link opens in a new window
    A great website providing detailed tips to visiting archives around the world, including practical matters such as transport and computer access.
  • Using Archives: A Guide to Effective Research This link opens in a new window
    This guide by the Society for American Archivists can help you understand how archives function, how to identify appropriate archives for your research, and how to conduct research in archives.
